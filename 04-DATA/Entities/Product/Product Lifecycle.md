# Product Lifecycle

## Purpose

The Product Lifecycle defines the complete journey of a Product throughout the ETA Enterprise Ecosystem, from initial creation to retirement.

It ensures standardized engineering governance, procurement readiness, inventory control, commercial management, and AI lifecycle support.

---

# Lifecycle Overview

```
Idea

↓

Draft

↓

Engineering Review

↓

Procurement Review

↓

Compliance Review

↓

Approval

↓

Active Product

↓

Operational Use

↓

Revision

↓

Obsolete

↓

Archived
```

---

# Stage 1 — Idea

A new Product requirement is identified from:

- Customer Request
- RFQ
- Market Opportunity
- Engineering Initiative
- AI Recommendation
- Supplier Proposal

Status

```
Idea
```

---

# Stage 2 — Draft

Initial Product master data is created.

Required information includes:

- Product Name
- Product Category
- Manufacturer
- Product Type

Status

```
Draft
```

---

# Stage 3 — Engineering Review

Engineering validates:

- Technical Specifications
- Standards
- Drawings
- Datasheets
- Compatibility
- Product Classification

Status

```
Engineering Review
```

---

# Stage 4 — Procurement Review

Procurement validates:

- Approved Suppliers
- Approved Manufacturers
- Lead Time
- Availability
- Pricing
- MOQ

Status

```
Procurement Review
```

---

# Stage 5 — Compliance Review

Compliance validates:

- Export Restrictions
- Certifications
- Regulatory Requirements
- Country Restrictions
- Quality Documents

Status

```
Compliance Review
```

---

# Stage 6 — Approval

Approval requires:

- Engineering
- Procurement
- Compliance

High-risk products may additionally require Executive approval.

Status

```
Approved
```

---

# Stage 7 — Active Product

The Product becomes available for:

- RFQs
- Quotations
- Purchase Orders
- Inventory
- Projects
- Contracts
- AI Search

Status

```
Active
```

---

# Stage 8 — Operational Use

The Product is actively used in:

- Procurement
- Sales
- Warehousing
- Logistics
- Projects
- Maintenance

Performance metrics are continuously monitored.

---

# Stage 9 — Revision

Major changes trigger a new revision.

Examples include:

- Technical Specification Changes
- New Manufacturer
- Material Changes
- Certification Updates
- Engineering Improvements

Previous revisions remain available for audit.

Status

```
Revision
```

---

# Stage 10 — Obsolete

Products become Obsolete due to:

- Manufacturer Discontinuation
- Engineering Replacement
- Technology Changes
- Commercial Decisions

Existing historical transactions remain unchanged.

Status

```
Obsolete
```

---

# Stage 11 — Archived

Archived Products remain available for:

- Historical Reporting
- Audit
- Traceability
- AI Knowledge
- Engineering Reference

Status

```
Archived
```

---

# AI Lifecycle Support

AI assists throughout the lifecycle by providing:

- Product Classification
- Technical Matching
- Alternative Products
- Supplier Recommendations
- Manufacturer Recommendations
- Demand Forecasting
- Risk Analysis
- Lifecycle Insights

---

# KPI Monitoring

Lifecycle KPIs include:

- Time to Approval
- Engineering Review Time
- Procurement Review Time
- Active Product Count
- Obsolete Product Ratio
- Revision Frequency
- Product Utilization
- Inventory Turnover

---

# Long-Term Vision

The Product Lifecycle becomes the enterprise governance model for every commercial and engineering item within ETA, ensuring full traceability, controlled revisions, ERP integration, AI intelligence, and standardized lifecycle management.
