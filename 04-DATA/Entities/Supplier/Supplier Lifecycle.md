# Supplier Lifecycle

## Purpose

The Supplier Lifecycle defines the complete journey of a supplier within the ETA Enterprise Ecosystem, from initial registration to archival.

This lifecycle ensures consistent onboarding, compliance, performance evaluation, and long-term supplier governance.

# Stage 1 — Prospect

Potential supplier identified through:

- Market Research
- Trade Shows
- Customer Recommendation
- Manufacturer Referral
- AI Supplier Discovery

Status

```
Prospect
```

---

# Stage 2 — Registration

Supplier basic information is collected.

Required information includes:

- Company Name
- Country
- Contact Information
- Registration Number
- Tax Information

Status

```
Registration
```

---

# Stage 3 — Qualification

ETA evaluates supplier capability.

Evaluation includes:

- Product Portfolio
- Manufacturing Capability
- Engineering Capability
- Financial Capacity
- Export Capability

Status

```
Qualification
```

---

# Stage 4 — Compliance Review

Compliance checks include:

- KYC
- AML
- Sanction Screening
- Certifications
- ISO Verification

Status

```
Compliance Review
```

---

# Stage 5 — Commercial Review

Commercial evaluation includes:

- Pricing
- Payment Terms
- Incoterms
- Delivery Capability
- Commercial Flexibility

Status

```
Commercial Review
```

---

# Stage 6 — Technical Evaluation

Engineering team validates:

- Technical Documents
- Datasheets
- Standards
- QA/QC
- Product Quality

Status

```
Technical Review
```

---

# Stage 7 — Approval

Procurement management approves supplier.

Approvers may include:

- Procurement Manager
- Commercial Manager
- Finance
- Executive

Status

```
Approved
```

---

# Stage 8 — Active Supplier

Supplier becomes available for:

- RFQs
- Quotations
- Purchase Orders
- Contracts
- Projects

Status

```
Active
```

---

# Stage 9 — Performance Monitoring

Supplier KPIs include:

- Delivery Performance
- Quality
- Commercial Response
- Lead Time
- Contract Compliance
- Financial Reliability

AI continuously evaluates supplier performance.

---

# Stage 10 — Preferred Supplier

Requirements:

- High Performance
- Low Risk
- Excellent Delivery
- Commercial Stability

Status

```
Preferred
```

---

# Stage 11 — Strategic Supplier

Strategic suppliers receive:

- Priority RFQs
- Long-term Contracts
- Joint Planning
- Partnership Programs

Status

```
Strategic
```

---

# Stage 12 — Suspension

Reasons include:

- Compliance Failure
- Financial Risk
- Delivery Failure
- Poor Quality
- Contract Breach

Status

```
Suspended
```

No new procurement activities are allowed.

---

# Stage 13 — Archive

Archived suppliers remain available for:

- Audit
- Reporting
- Historical Procurement
- AI Learning

No operational activity is permitted.

Status

```
Archived
```
