# Manufacturer Lifecycle

## Purpose

The Manufacturer Lifecycle defines the complete journey of an Original Equipment Manufacturer (OEM) within the ETA Enterprise Ecosystem.

It ensures standardized onboarding, engineering validation, compliance, performance monitoring, and strategic manufacturer governance.

---

# Lifecycle Overview

```
Prospect

↓

Registration

↓

Engineering Evaluation

↓

Compliance Review

↓

Commercial Review

↓

Approval

↓

Active Manufacturer

↓

Performance Monitoring

↓

Preferred Manufacturer

↓

Strategic Manufacturer

↓

Suspended

↓

Archived
```

---

# Stage 1 — Prospect

Potential manufacturers are identified through:

- Market Research
- Industry Directories
- OEM Databases
- Trade Shows
- AI Discovery
- Customer Requirements

Status

```
Prospect
```

---

# Stage 2 — Registration

Basic manufacturer information is collected.

Required information includes:

- Company Name
- Country
- Factory Location
- Registration Number
- Website
- Engineering Contacts

Status

```
Registration
```

---

# Stage 3 — Engineering Evaluation

Engineering validates:

- Product Portfolio
- Manufacturing Capability
- Production Capacity
- Quality Systems
- Technical Standards
- Testing Facilities

Status

```
Engineering Review
```

---

# Stage 4 — Compliance Review

Compliance validates:

- KYC
- AML
- Export Restrictions
- Certifications
- ISO Standards
- Regulatory Compliance

Status

```
Compliance Review
```

---

# Stage 5 — Commercial Review

Commercial evaluates:

- Market Position
- Commercial Flexibility
- Delivery Capability
- Warranty Policy
- Pricing Competitiveness
- Global Support

Status

```
Commercial Review
```

---

# Stage 6 — Approval

Approval is performed by:

- Engineering Manager
- Procurement Manager
- Commercial Manager
- Executive Management

Status

```
Approved
```

---

# Stage 7 — Active Manufacturer

The manufacturer becomes available for:

- Product Assignment
- RFQ Participation
- Strategic Procurement
- OEM Evaluation
- Engineering Selection

Status

```
Active
```

---

# Stage 8 — Performance Monitoring

ETA continuously evaluates:

- Product Quality
- Technical Support
- Manufacturing Performance
- Lead Time
- Delivery Reliability
- Certification Status

AI continuously updates manufacturer intelligence.

---

# Stage 9 — Preferred Manufacturer

Requirements include:

- High Product Quality
- Stable Manufacturing
- Low Risk
- Strong Engineering Support
- Excellent Delivery History

Status

```
Preferred
```

---

# Stage 10 — Strategic Manufacturer

Strategic manufacturers receive:

- Preferred Engineering Status
- Strategic Procurement Priority
- Long-Term Agreements
- Joint Development Opportunities
- Executive Visibility

Status

```
Strategic
```

---

# Stage 11 — Suspension

Reasons include:

- Compliance Failure
- Certification Expiration
- Production Issues
- Sanctions
- Serious Quality Problems

Status

```
Suspended
```

No new sourcing activities are permitted.

---

# Stage 12 — Archive

Archived manufacturers remain available for:

- Historical Reporting
- Product Traceability
- Audit
- AI Knowledge
- Engineering History

Status

```
Archived
```
