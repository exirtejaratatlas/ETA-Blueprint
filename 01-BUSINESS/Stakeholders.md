# Enterprise Stakeholders

## Purpose

This document defines every stakeholder interacting with the ETA Enterprise Ecosystem.

Stakeholders include people, organizations, departments, external partners, AI agents, and integrated systems that create, consume, approve, analyze, or manage business information.

This document is the foundation for:

- Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
- Permission Matrix
- Workflow Design
- Approval Matrix
- Notification Rules
- Dashboard Design
- AI Agents
- Security Architecture

---

# Stakeholder Categories

ETA stakeholders are divided into six major groups:

- Executive Stakeholders
- Internal Operational Stakeholders
- External Business Stakeholders
- Technical Stakeholders
- AI Stakeholders
- Integrated Systems

# Stakeholder Relationships

Every stakeholder interacts with ETA through:

- Role-Based Access Control
- Workflow Engine
- Approval Matrix
- Audit Logs
- Notifications
- AI Services

No stakeholder may access information outside their assigned permissions.

# Stakeholder Lifecycle

Every stakeholder follows a common lifecycle:

1. Registration
2. Verification
3. Activation
4. Daily Operations
5. Monitoring
6. Performance Evaluation
7. Deactivation (if required)
