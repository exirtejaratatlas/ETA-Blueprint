# Compliance Framework

# Purpose

This document defines the enterprise compliance framework governing all financial, commercial and regulatory activities within the ETA Enterprise Business Operating System.

ETA is designed for an Iranian B2B procurement and trading enterprise operating in domestic and international markets.

Compliance is treated as a core business capability rather than an after-the-fact control mechanism.

---

# Compliance Objectives

The framework ensures:

- Legal Compliance
- Financial Compliance
- Tax Compliance
- Commercial Compliance
- Contract Compliance
- Procurement Compliance
- Import & Export Compliance
- Internal Control
- Audit Readiness
- AI Governance

---

# Compliance Principles

ETA follows the following principles:

- Compliance by Design
- Zero Trust
- Segregation of Duties
- Full Traceability
- Least Privilege
- Continuous Monitoring
- Versioned Business Rules
- Explainable Decisions

# Procurement Compliance

Every procurement activity must verify:

- Approved Supplier
- Approved Budget
- Approval Authority
- Contract Availability
- Purchase Policy
- Risk Assessment

No Purchase Order may be issued without satisfying these controls.

# AI Compliance

AI Agents must:

- Explain recommendations
- Never bypass approvals
- Respect Business Rules
- Produce audit logs
- Avoid autonomous financial execution

All AI actions remain advisory unless explicitly approved.

# Business Rules Engine

Compliance decisions are driven by configurable business rules.

Examples:

IF Supplier Status = Suspended

THEN Reject Purchase Order

---

IF Invoice exceeds Approval Limit

THEN Require Executive Approval

---

IF VAT Registration Invalid

THEN Reject Tax Credit

---

IF Contract Expired

THEN Block Payment
